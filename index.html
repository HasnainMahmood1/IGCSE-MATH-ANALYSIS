Percentage of 200 marks required for A*. June = teal, November = orange. Missing sessions shown as gaps.

📉June 2022 — All-time low (BX)

June 2022 produced the lowest BX A* ever at 133/200 (66.5%). This is extraordinary — 27 marks below June 2023. Paper 41 was notably accessible (Component 41 A threshold: just 67/130). BY/BZ were more typical at 159/154, showing the BX–BY gap reached its widest at 26 marks.

📈November 2017 — All-time high (BY)

November 2017's BY option required 189/200 (94.5%) for A* — the highest threshold in this dataset. BX also hit 179 (89.5%). Papers 22 and 42 were set exceptionally hard that year, with Component 22 alone requiring 64/70 for an A (91%).

📐BY nearly always hardest

The BY option (Papers 22 + 42) requires the most marks for A* in almost every session. The average BY–BX gap is around 12–15 marks. BZ is usually intermediate. The exception is June 2021 where BY was the easiest at 139 — a COVID-adjusted anomaly.

💡Safe A* target

Targeting ≥160/200 (80%) on Extended provides a safe A* buffer in virtually every session. The absolute floor was BX Jun 2022 at 66.5%. The ceiling was BY Nov 2017 at 94.5%. For BY specifically, aim for 170+ (85%) to be safe across all years.

📊June 2022 — Anomalous BX low

June 2022 BX A* was just 133/200 (66.5%) — by far the lowest in any session. Component 41 (Paper 4 Variant 1) A threshold was only 67/130 (51.5%), suggesting an unusually hard paper. BY (159) and BZ (154) in the same session were normal, confirming it was variant-specific.

📊June 2021 — BY becomes easiest

Uniquely, June 2021 saw BY (139) lower than both BX (154) and BZ (148) — the only session in the dataset where BY is not the hardest. A* thresholds across all variants were the lowest in any June session, due to COVID-19 grading adjustments.

⚡Nov 2017 — Record BY threshold

November 2017 set the record: BY at 189/200 (94.5%) and BX at 179/200 (89.5%). Component 22 required 64/70 for A (91.4%) — almost perfect scores needed. This is the hardest session on record for any option, and reflects Papers 22/42 being exceptionally demanding that year.

📉Post-2020 normalisation

After Nov 2020's COVID-adjusted lower thresholds, November BX recovered to a high of 173 in 2023. The 2022–2025 period shows more stable BX thresholds (154–173) compared to the high volatility of 2015–2019. November BY has been consistently above 169 since 2022.

📈November consistently harder than June

In most years, November BX thresholds exceed the corresponding June BX. The average gap is around 15–20 marks (7–10%). Exceptions: 2021 (COVID; June harder for BY) and 2023 (virtually identical at 158 vs 173). This pattern suggests November papers are routinely more demanding or the marking is stricter.

📅High volatility year-to-year

IGCSE Maths shows extreme year-to-year swings — more than most subjects. BX jumped from 133 (Jun 2022) to 158 (Jun 2023) — a 25-mark rise in one year. BY in November went from 165 (Nov 2020) to 170 (Nov 2022). BZ in November 2017 was just 166 vs BY 189 in the same session — a 23-mark gap between variants.

🔄BY–BX spread: consistent but volatile

The BY–BX gap averages about 12 marks in June and 10 marks in November. But it's been as large as 26 marks (Jun 2022: BY=159, BX=133) and as small as −15 marks (Jun 2021: BY=139, BX=154). Students on BY almost always need significantly more marks — except during COVID adjustments.

💡2025 syllabus change (new paper weights)

From 2025, component weights changed: Paper 2 and 4 now carry 100 marks each (previously 70+130). The total remains 200. June 2025 BX=156, BY=176, BZ=178. November 2025 BX=172, BY=169, BZ=177. Thresholds are broadly comparable to the pre-2025 format despite different raw mark distributions.
